Sitatunga
Tragelaphus spekei gratus
Order: artiodactyls
Family: bovinae
Characteristics
Sitatunga has elongated hooves, ankle flexibility, so that copes well in wetlands and wetland.
Lifestyle
He lives alone, in pairs or small groups.
Breeding
After the birth of young hiding on a dry clump of tall reeds.
Nesting site
Wetlands and humid equatorial forests around lakes and rivers.
Protection
Threats: Drainage of wetlands.
