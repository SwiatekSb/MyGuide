Guanaco
Lama guanicoe
Order: artiodactyls
Family: camelids
Characteristics
Body length 153-200 cm, height at the withers 90-125 cm.
Lifestyle
It lives in small flocks, consisting of a maximum of 20-30 individuals.
Breeding
After a pregnancy lasting 11 months the female gives birth to one young, who partakes only of milk for 4 months.
Nesting site
Drylands and semi-arid savanna.
Protection
Threats: Protected species and disappearing due to habitat destruction and hunting due to the high quality of the skin.
Interesting facts
  • They are considered as the ancestor of the llama, people in ancient times Gwanko hunted for their meat and fur.
