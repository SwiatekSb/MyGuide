Antelope Kama
Alcelaphus caama
Order: artiodactyls
Family: bovinae
Characteristics
English name of these antelopes comes from the Dutch word, hart ", meaning, strength" and was given by the first European settlers.
Lifestyle
They create a herd, numbering from 20 to 300 individuals.
Breeding
In some territories, animals reproduce only during the rainy season.
Nesting site
Savannah.
Protection
Threats: Their number decreases due to hunting (they are easy prey and competition with cattle, because they are less selective in choosing grass species).
Interesting facts
  • Antelope Kama is one of the fastest antelope in Africa.
