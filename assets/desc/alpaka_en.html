Alpaca
Vicugna Pacos
Order: artiodactyls
Family: camelids
Characteristics
Body alpaca reaches 128-151 cm long, with a height of 80-100 cm at the withers and weighing 55-65 kg.
Lifestyle
They are social animals forming family groups consisting of a dominant male, several females and their offspring.
Breeding
Pregnancy in alpaca takes about 335-340 days, usually born one small.
Nesting site
Located high mountain plateaus.
Protection
Risks: Because of their wool, considered one of the best in the world, hunted these animals.
Interesting facts
  • Farmers in the UK use the alpaca to defend the sheep from predators.
